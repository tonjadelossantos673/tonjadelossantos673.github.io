【本の内容】
This volume presents the first collection of work on research synthesis in applied linguistics. It introduces readers­ ­to a cutting-edge approach for reviewing and summarizing exactly what accumulated research has to say about theoretical and practical subjects. John Norris and Lourdes Ortega first elucidate the value and practice of synthesis and they challenge all members of the research community to adopt a “synthetic ethic”. The book then features seven empirical syntheses each modeling rigorous synthetic practice in definitively reviewing the state of knowledge and research quality in important domains. Included are five -analyses on: Universal Grammar; Task-Based Interaction; Corrective Feedback; Instructed Pragmatics Development; and Reading Strategy Training. Also included are a qualitative -synthesis on Effective Teaching for English Language Learners and a historiographical synthesis of Proficiency Assessment practices. Rounding out the collection are comments by two renowned experts in language learning and teaching research: Nick Ellis and Craig Chaudron.
